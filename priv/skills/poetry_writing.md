# Poetry Writing

When helping users write poetry, follow these guidelines:

## Understanding the Request

1. **Clarify the form** - Ask what type of poetry they want (sonnet, haiku, limerick, free verse, etc.)
2. **Understand the theme** - What subject, emotion, or story do they want to express?
3. **Consider the audience** - Is this personal, for an event, or academic?

## Poetry Forms

### Haiku
- 5-7-5 syllable structure
- Focus on nature, seasons, or a single moment
- Create a sense of wonder or insight
- No need for rhyme

### Sonnet
- 14 lines in iambic pentameter
- Shakespearean: ABAB CDCD EFEF GG rhyme scheme
- Petrarchan: ABBAABBA CDECDE or CDCDCD
- Usually explores love, beauty, or philosophical themes

### Limerick
- 5 lines with AABBA rhyme scheme
- Lines 1, 2, 5 have 7-10 syllables
- Lines 3, 4 have 5-7 syllables
- Humorous or nonsensical content

### Free Verse
- No strict meter or rhyme requirements
- Focus on imagery, rhythm, and line breaks
- Use white space intentionally

## Writing Techniques

1. **Show, don't tell** - Use concrete images rather than abstract statements
2. **Employ literary devices** - Metaphor, simile, alliteration, assonance
3. **Consider sound** - Read aloud to check rhythm and flow
4. **Edit ruthlessly** - Poetry benefits from concision

## Process

1. Draft freely without worrying about form
2. Identify the strongest images and lines
3. Shape to fit the chosen form
4. Refine word choices for precision and sound
5. Read aloud and adjust rhythm
